Investing 101: A Guide to Cryptocurrency

Planning to invest in cryptocurrency? Here are 3 options you can try out :)

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency, created and stored electronically, that operates without the need for a central authority, such as a government or bank. It is based on blockchain technology, which is a distributed ledger that records all transactions across a network of computers. Bitcoin transactions are verified by network nodes through cryptography and recorded in the blockchain, ensuring security and transparency.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A utility token is a type of cryptocurrency that provides users with access to a specific product or service within a blockchain-based ecosystem or platform. Unlike security tokens, which represent ownership or investment in an asset, utility tokens are primarily used to facilitate certain functions or operations within a given platform. Utility tokens are an important part of many blockchain-based projects and serve as a fundamental mechanism for users to engage with and use decentralized systems.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Litecoin (LTC) is a peer-to-peer cryptocurrency that was created by Charlie Lee in 2011 as a "lite" version of Bitcoin. Litecoin is primarily used as a medium of exchange for goods and services, as well as a store of value. Due to its faster transaction speed and lower transaction fees, it is sometimes preferred for microtransactions or small purchases compared to Bitcoin. It also acts as a testbed for some new technologies and features that may eventually be adopted by Bitcoin, such as the Lightning Network or Segregated Witness (SegWit), which Litecoin implemented before Bitcoin.
